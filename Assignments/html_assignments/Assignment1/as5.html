Masala Dosa

Masala dosa is a famous and satisfying South Indian dish that's perfect for a hearty breakfast or a light dinner. It consists of a crispy, savory crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter, wrapped around a delicious, spicy potato filling.

Ingredients:

  • Dosa Batter (pre-made or homemade)
  • Potatoes
    • Alternative for stuffing: You can also use finely chopped carrots or peas.
  • Onions
  • Green chilies
  • Mustard seeds
  • Curry leaves
  • Oil
  • Salt to taste

Cooking Steps:

  1. Heat a small amount of oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, letting them splutter.
  2. Add curry leaves, chopped onions, and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn translucent.
  3. Add boiled and mashed potatoes along with salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  4. Heat a non-stick tawa (griddle) and pour a ladle of dosa batter onto it.
  5. Spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin crepe. Drizzle a little oil around the edges.
  6. Once the dosa turns golden and crispy, place the potato filling in the center.
  7. Fold the dosa in half and serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Cooking Terms:

Sauté
To cook food quickly in a small amount of hot fat or oil over high heat.
Garnish
To decorate a dish with a small amount of another food to improve its aesthetic appeal.

Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala is a rich and creamy North Indian curry that is a favorite in households and restaurants alike. It features soft cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a tomato, butter, and cream-based gravy.

Ingredients:

  • Paneer cubes
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Butter
  • Heavy cream
  • Cashews or Almonds
    • Optional: A few tablespoons of coconut milk can be used for a dairy-free version.
  • Spices:
    • Garam Masala
    • Chili Powder
    • Coriander Powder

Cooking Steps:

  1. Chop the onions and tomatoes. Prepare a smooth puree by blending them with soaked cashews.
  2. In a pan, melt butter and add the tomato-onion puree. Cook until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Stir and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the paneer cubes and some water to achieve the desired consistency. Simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream and a little more butter. Season with salt and sugar if needed.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving hot with naan or rice.

Cooking Terms:

Simmer
To cook a liquid just below the boiling point, typically with small bubbles forming at the surface.
Puree
A smooth, thick liquid or paste made by blending fruits, vegetables, or other food items.

Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian dessert cherished across the country for its melt-in-the-mouth texture. These fried milk-solid dumplings are soaked in a fragrant sugar syrup, infused with rose water or cardamom.

Ingredients:

  • Khoya (milk solids)
  • Maida (all-purpose flour)
  • Baking soda
  • Ghee or Oil for frying
  • For the Sugar Syrup:
    • Sugar
    • Water
    • Cardamom pods
    • Rose water

Cooking Steps:

  1. In a bowl, knead the khoya and maida together to form a smooth, soft dough. Add a pinch of baking soda.
  2. Divide the dough into small portions and gently roll them into smooth, crack-free balls.
  3. Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan over low-medium heat.
  4. Carefully add the gulab jamun balls and deep-fry until they are golden brown all over.
  5. While frying, prepare the syrup by boiling sugar, water, and cardamom pods until it becomes slightly sticky.
  6. Carefully place the hot gulab jamun balls into the warm sugar syrup.
  7. Let them soak for at least 30 minutes to absorb the syrup. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Cooking Terms:

Knead
To work a dough or paste by hand to mix ingredients and develop consistency.
Syrup
A thick, sweet liquid made by dissolving sugar in boiling water, often flavored with spices or extracts.
